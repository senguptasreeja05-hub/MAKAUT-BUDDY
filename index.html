INTRODUCTION
In the present academic environment, students of Maulana Abul Kalam Azad University of Technology (MAKAUT) often face difficulties in finding authentic and well-organized study resources such as previous years’ question papers, solutions, syllabus-wise reference videos, and handwritten notes. These resources are usually scattered across multiple platforms, making preparation time-consuming and inefficient. As a result, students spend more effort searching for materials rather than focusing on effective learning and exam preparation. This project aims to develop a MAKAUT Academic Resource and Doubt Management System that provides a centralized platform for students to access year-wise question papers with solutions, syllabus-wise curated YouTube video links, and handwritten notes in a structured manner. The system is designed to help students understand the syllabus clearly, revise important topics efficiently, and prepare systematically for examinations. The project also includes a basic doubt-clearing mechanism, allowing students to submit academic doubts related to specific subjects or topics. This feature encourages interactive learning and helps students overcome conceptual difficulties in a timely manner. The proposed system focuses on simplicity, usability, and academic relevance. It is designed keeping in mind the needs of MAKAUT students and follows a modular approach for easy data management and future expansion. In the future, the system can be extended into a full-fledged web or mobile application with advanced features such as user authentication, live doubt-clearing, and personalized recommendations.


OBJECTIVES OF THE PROJECT

The main objectives of this project are as follows:

  • To design and develop a centralized academic resource system for MAKAUT students.
  • To provide year-wise access to previous years,MAKAUT question papers for effective exam preparation>
  • To include solutions to question papers to help students understand the correct approach to answering questions.
  • To organize and present syllabus-wise YouTube video links for each subject and unit.
  • To provide access to handwritten notes for quick revision and better conceptual clarity.
  • To develop a structured system that allows easy search and retrieval of academic resources.
  • To implement a basic doubt submission mechanism to support students in resolving academic queries.
  • To improve learning efficiency by reducing the time spent searching for study materials.
  • To design the system in a modular manner for easy maintenance and future enhancement.


Scope of the project
The project focuses on organizing and providing academic resources for MAKAUT students in a simple, structured system with provisions for future expansion.
